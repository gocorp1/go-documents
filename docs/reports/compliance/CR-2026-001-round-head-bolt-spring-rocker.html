Pass
The visible round-head fastener configuration is a recognised compliant design under ASTM F1487-17 §6.2.1, §6.2.4, §6.3, and §8.11.2 provided that (a) every fastener head, when the rubber/plastic protective cap is fully seated, does not extend beyond the face of any of the three projection test gauges (1.25 in., 2.0 in., and 3.5 in. OD — F1487 Fig. A1.10), (b) no cap is displaced or missing, and (c) no metal head, shoulder, or thread is accessible to a user. One cap in the field photograph appears partially displaced — see Finding F-2. Pass is conditional on remedial actions R-1 & R-2 (page 2).

Findings

F-1 — Round-head fastener selection (design): Use of round-head (button-head) machine fasteners with continuous-taper protective caps is the manufacturer-preferred, compliant treatment for visible attachment points on spring rocking equipment. The selection is consistent with F1487-17 §6.2.1 (no sharp edges/points), §6.4.4 (no size-increasing projection), and the CPSC #325 owner guidance to keep accessible hardware flush or capped. No design change required.

F-2 — Cap seating (maintenance): On the lower of the two highlighted fasteners, the protective cap appears partially displaced — a thin metal rim is visible at the cap-to-panel interface. While the cap material itself is not sharp, a displaced cap (i) may admit a 1.25 in. projection gauge to register a measurable head projection that an in-situ seated cap would not, and (ii) exposes a metal edge that is no longer flush with the panel surface, contrary to CPSC #325 §5.3.5.2 and LEKA-STD-PLAY-001 §5.3.

F-3 — Thread end (rear of panel, not visible in photo): F1487 §6.4.3 prohibits any accessible bolt end projecting beyond the face of the nut by more than two full threads. The rear (nut) side of the green panel was not visible in the submitted evidence. The rear face must be inspected separately to confirm thread protrusion compliance.

F-4 — In-field projection gauge test required: Definitive pass/fail under F1487 §6.3 can only be rendered with the physical 1.25 in., 2.0 in., and 3.5 in. OD projection gauges (Fig. A1.10) placed over the head in all orientations. Photographic estimation indicates a likely pass but is not binding.

Risk Classification (LEKA-STD-PLAY-001 §13)
  • F-1 — Class IV (cosmetic / no risk).
  • F-2 — Class III (minor injury). Loose protective cap. Remediation within 30 days; document in maintenance log.
  • F-3 — pending; classification depends on observed thread projection at rear of panel.
  • F-4 — procedural; no risk class until physical test completed.
Required Remediation
  • R-1 (F-2): Reseat or replace the displaced protective cap on the lower fastener of the inspected side. Use the original-equipment cap part. If the cap is degraded, replace both caps on that handle so the appearance and retention force match. Confirm cap retention with a manual pull test (cap shall not separate under reasonable hand force per the manufacturer's specification).
  • R-2 (F-2): Inspect every spring rocker handle attachment on the same site for cap displacement; remediate any others found.
  • R-3 (F-3): Inspect the rear (nut side) of the green panel. If any thread end projects more than two full threads beyond the face of the nut, trim flush with a hand file (chamfer end — do not generate burrs per F1487 §6.2.4) or fit a domed acorn nut sized to fully enclose the thread.
  • R-4 (F-4): Perform the F1487 §6.3 projection gauge test in-field with all three gauges over every accessible fastener head; record results in the inspection log. Re-issue this review with a final pass/fail.
  • R-5 (preventive): Add a "protective cap retention check" line item to the high-frequency (daily / weekly) inspection checklist for all Leka spring rocking equipment (LEKA-STD-PLAY-001 §11).
